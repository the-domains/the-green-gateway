Carbon Brief: U.K. Solar Generation Tops Coal for the First Time The U.K.'s solar panels have generated more electricity than coal in a full day for the first time ever, Carbon Brief analysis shows. On Saturday 9 April, solar generated 29 gigawatt-hours (GWh) of electricity, 4% of the total used that day and more than the 21 GWh output from coal (3% of demand).
